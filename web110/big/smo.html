Social Media Optimization (SMO)

Worldwide, 3.7 billion people are on the internet, 2.7 billion are active on social media, and 2.5 billion use their phones for social media. According to the Pew Research Center, a majority of Americans are now using social media on a daily basis. With so many people on social media platforms every day, it’s likely that your customers and potential customers are among them.

Unlike other forms of advertising, social media allows companies to reach customers regularly with entertaining, educational, and promotional messaging they can respond to directly. Some common social media strategic goals are: improving customer engagement, generating more leads, growing sales, increasing traffic, expanding reach, and enhancing brand perception.

Part of a good social media strategy is determining which channels make the most sense for your business to use to reach your customers. Having an impact on a social network and driving business value from your presence there requires a big investment of time and resources. Most social networks provide data on their own audience’s demographics which will help determine whether or not that channel makes sense to your business. Are you targeting an older demographic? Then having and maintaining a snapchat or tik tok account may not be right for your business.

One way to get the attention of your customers is by communicating on the most popular social networks with the largest active audiences and the widest range of business use cases. Because of their high number of monthly active users, Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, and YouTube should be top considerations when choosing which channels are right for you.

Keeping your social media profiles current, engaging with your customers, providing content relevant to your demographic, and providing links to your social media accounts as well as the ability to directly share content from your website to the user’s social media accounts can all help to successfully market and grow your brand.
